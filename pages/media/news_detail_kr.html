Hyundai Heavy, Onshore demonstration of HiMSEN Engine using bio-fuel targeting the Greenhouse gas reduction on ships

06.21.2021
faceBook twitter kakaotalk
Attachments
img_02.jpg

On Monday, March 15th, HHI (Hyundai Heavy Industries) and KSOE (Korea shipbuilding & Offshore Engineering), invited the biggest Korean Container shipping company HMM(Hyundai Merchant Marine), KR(Korean Register) and Korea Bio-energy Association to perform demonstration test for HiMSEN Engine using bio-fuel to reduce Greenhouse gas.

International Maritime Organization (IMO) informed to be planned to extend application of CO2 emissions regulation to the existing ships from 2023. Europe Union (EU) said that, including emission trading scheme, this demonstration test will be more meaningful for shipbuilding and maritime industry in terms of using Bio-fuel as alternative fuel among reducing GHG emission the strategies.

Bio-fuel is mixture from bio-heavy oil and Low sulfur fuel oil made by scrapped bio resources such as animal-vegetable oil, process byproduct of bio diesel, used cooking oil and etc. Bio-heavy oil has been used for domestic power plant using heavy fuel oil, and its production capacity is approximately 200,000 tons. Bio-Heavy oil is regarded as a carbon neutral fuel that has not GHG increase in the atmosphere, and it is eco-friendly fuel as well in terms of sulfuric acid (SOx) emission can be reduced greatly.

In this event, the HiMSEN Engine using bio-heavy fuel was operated in the main operational condition of ships. And it showed not only the reduced GHG and air pollutant emissions but equivalent performance comparing that of using normal fuel oil.

This demonstration test was performed as follow up measure of MOU agreed between HHI groups, HMM, KR, Korea Bio-energy Association regarding Bio-fuel oil vessel last year. And this onshore demonstration test was performed about HiMSEN engine in the Hyundai Heavy Industries' Eco-friendly demonstration center. This HiMSEN engine demonstration is expected to be a great help to realization of eco-friendly vessels in the future. In this year, the institutes attending this event are planning to cooperate continuously to reduce the ship greenhouse gas as promoting the demonstration of the 13,100 TEU Container vessels using bio-fuel of HMM.
